1. Who can donate blood?

Anyone in good health, aged 16 or 17 and older (depending on state laws), weighing at least 110 lbs (50 kg), and meeting other health criteria can donate. Parental consent may be required for donors under 18.

2. How often can I donate blood?

  • Whole blood: Every 56 days (up to 6 times per year).
  • Power Red: Every 112 days (up to 3 times per year).
  • Platelets: Every 7 days, up to 24 times per year.

Yes! Sterile, single-use equipment is used for each donor. Blood donation is a safe process regulated by health authorities to protect both donors and recipients.
